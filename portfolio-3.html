Project Overview

Responsive web

The Estate Help Dashboard serves as a powerful and intuitive platform for efficiently managing resident reports, tracking issue statuses, and responding to feedback in real time. In many housing communities, estate managers rely on fragmented systems or manual tracking, which can lead to delays, lost reports, and a lack of transparency. EstateHelp solves this by centralizing all incoming reports in a single interface—allowing admins to view, filter, categorize, and update the status of each issue with ease.

Project information

  • Category: Responsive Website design
  • Project Duration: March 2025- June, 2025

PROBLEM

The main problem for estate administrators is the lack of a centralized and efficient system to manage and track user-reported issues, feedback, and notifications. Traditional methods—like handwritten logs, spreadsheets, or verbal reports—are often disorganized, slow, and prone to miscommunication or data loss. This leads to delayed responses, unresolved complaints, and frustration among residents.

THE GOAL

The goal of the Estate Help Dashboard is to provide estate administrators with an efficient, user-friendly platform that simplifies the process of managing resident-reported issues, feedback, and notifications. It aims to centralize all incoming reports, track their progress in real-time, and allow for easy prioritization and resolution.

UNDERSTANDING THE USER

User research: Summary

Conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand users' needs, behaviors, and motivations. My goal for conducting primary research was to identify the user pain points.

User Research: Pain Point

01

Disorganized Issue Tracking

Many estate administrators struggle with fragmented tracking systems, including handwritten logs, spreadsheets, or multiple communication channels. This leads to confusion over the status of tasks, delays in responding to issues, and difficulty in prioritizing urgent requests.

02

Lack of Real-Time Updates

Without a centralized platform, admins often miss out on real-time notifications about the status of issues. This lack of visibility can delay responses, causing frustration for residents who are left wondering about the progress of their requests.

03

Complex User Interface

Estate admins have difficulty navigating platforms that are overly complex or unintuitive. Admins may need to spend unnecessary time searching for features, which slows down their ability to address issues quickly and efficiently.

Accessibility Considerations

  1. Clear and Consistent Navigation:Maintain consistent layout and navigation structure.
  2. Form Accessibility:Ensure form fields have associated labels, use error messages that are descriptive, and include keyboard-accessible dropdowns and checkboxes.
  3. Descriptive Link Text:Use meaningful text for links (e.g., “View Plant Details” instead of “Click Here”) so users understand where the link will take them.

Going Forward

Take away

This project highlighted how thoughtful UX design, informed by structured usability testing, can transform a digital tool from functional to effective, user-friendly, and impactful.

Next Step

Moving forward, I would conduct additional usability tests with a broader and more diverse group of users to uncover further pain points and edge cases. Based on feedback, I would iterate on the interface design—especially enhancing the filtering system, improving responsiveness, and adding tooltips or micro-interactions for better guidance.
