Project Overview

Responsive Mobile App

In today’s fast-paced world, people juggle multiple responsibilities, often leading to missed deadlines and increased stress. While paper to-do lists can help, they’re easy to lose and lack accessibility. A digital to-do list app offers a smarter solution—helping users manage tasks efficiently, set priorities and reminders, and access their list anytime, anywhere. The goal is to create a user-friendly, customizable app that simplifies task management, boosts productivity, and reduces stress.

Project information

PROBLEM

People are usually busy with various duties and responsibilities in today's fast-paced environment. While paper-based to-do lists can be useful, they are frequently disorganized, easily misplaced, and inaccessible on the go. As a result, there is a need for a digital to-do list software that may help users manage their activities more efficiently, prioritize them, make reminders, and access them from anywhere, at any time.

THE GOAL

To create an application that allows users to create tasks and reliably keep reminders and due dates.   

ROLE

UNDERSTANDING THE USER

User research: Summary

Conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand users' needs, behaviors, and motivations. My goal for conducting primary research was to identify the user pain points. The primary users were adults who were plant enthusiasts,casual online shoppers.

User Research: Pain Point

01

Overwhelming Task Lists

Users may feel overwhelmed when too many tasks are shown at once without categorization or prioritization. Lack of task hierarchy or grouping (e.g., personal vs. work) makes it hard to focus.

02

No Notifications or Reminders

Users miss deadlines because they don’t get timely reminders or alerts. Customizable notifications are often absent.

03

Poor User Interface (UI) / User Experience (UX)Uncertainty About Seller Legitimacy

Complex or cluttered UI can confuse users.Lack of intuitive navigation or too many steps to add/edit a task.

DESIGN

Paper Wireframe

I sketched paper wireframes of the homepage, login, signup ,task creation, and notification pages to explore layout options. I evaluated what worked best in terms of user experience and interface design, then translated those insights into digital wireframes to ensure a user-friendly Recipe app.

Digital Wireframe

I created digital wireframes in Figma for all screens. The design followed a clean layout, with a central homepage and bottom navigation for adding task,notification,status.

The prototype mapped the core user flow of the app. After finalizing digital wireframes, I added interactions and transitions to showcase seamless navigation between app screens—bringing the concept to life.

Accessibility Considerations

  1. Keyboard Navigation: All interactive elements (buttons, input fields, modals) are accessible using the keyboard alone, supporting smooth navigation with Tab, enter and arrow key.
  2. Color and Contrast: The app maintains a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for text against background colors, and uses icons and text to convey task statuses, not relying solely on color.
  3. Form Accessibility: Every form field is labeled clearly, with error messages announced to screen readers. Inputs also support proper tab order and focus management.

Going Forward

Take away

While working on the Task Scheduler App, I learned the fundamentals of UI/UX design, including how to create clean, intuitive interfaces that enhance user experience. I gained practical skills in organizing layouts, choosing appropriate colors and typography, and designing user flows that are simple and efficient. I also understood the importance of user feedback elements like confirmations, tooltips, and error messages. This project helped me appreciate the balance between visual design and functionality, and how thoughtful UI/UX choices directly impact usability and accessibility.

Next Step

To improve the Task Scheduler App, my next steps include adding data analytics features to track task completion rates and user activity. I also plan to implement offline support using service workers so users can access their tasks without an internet connection.
