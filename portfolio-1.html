Project Overview

Responsive Mobile App

This website acts as a digital storefront for plant shops, enhancing visibility to online-searching potential customers. It ensures a secure and trustworthy shopping environment, bolstering confidence in the brand identity and data security.

Project information

  • Category: Website design
  • Project Duration: March 2024 - June, 2024

PROBLEM

The growing popularity of social media has made running a plant shop more convenient. But it doesn't have the company identity, security, or reliability that users need.

THE GOAL

Efficient Ordering: Streamline the plant ordering process for easy browsing, selection, and purchase. Enhanced Experience: Ensure customer satisfaction with clear visuals and detailed descriptions.

ROLE

UX/UI Designer. [UI (user interface), UX (user experience)] Backend and frontend developer

Responsibilities

User research, User persona, Problem statement, User journey map,User flow, Storyboard, Low fidelity wireframe, Low fidelity prototypes, High fidelity mockups, High fidelity prototypes, Usability studies

UNDERSTANDING THE USER

User research: Summary

Conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand users' needs, behaviors, and motivations. My goal for conducting primary research was to identify the user pain points. The primary users were adults who were plant enthusiasts,casual online shoppers.

User Research: Pain Point

01

Limited Product Information

social media may not provide enough detailed product descriptions or reliable information about the plants being sold, leaving customers unsure about the quality or characteristics of what they’re purchasing.

02

Brand Visibility and Engagement

Without a digital platform, plant shops could have trouble reaching potential customers who primarily search online, limiting their growth potential and overall business reach.

03

Uncertainty About Seller Legitimacy

Social media platforms make it difficult for customers to verify the legitimacy of the plant seller, which could result in lost sales or lack of customer trust.

DESIGN

Paper Wireframe

I sketched paper wireframes of the homepage, login, signup ,Cart, and checkout pages to explore layout options. I evaluated what worked best in terms of user experience and interface design, then translated those insights into digital wireframes to ensure a user-friendly Recipe app.

Digital Wireframe

I created digital wireframes in Figma for all screens. The design followed a clean layout, with a central homepage and bottom navigation for searching,adding to cart ,detail view of the plant and checkout, the interface intuitive and accessible.

Low-Fidelity Prototypes

The prototype mapped the core user flow of the app. After finalizing digital wireframes, I added interactions and transitions to showcase seamless navigation between app screens—bringing the concept to life.

Usability Study

Tasks tested in usability study included:
1. Search and view a plant under different category
2. view plant in detail
3. Add plant to the cart
4. Proceed to checkout.
5. Locate contact section

Accessibility Considerations

  1. Clear and Consistent Navigation:Maintain consistent layout, navigation structure, and headings across all pages to help users with cognitive disabilities.
  2. Form Accessibility:Ensure form fields have associated labels, use error messages that are descriptive, and include keyboard-accessible dropdowns and checkboxes.
  3. Descriptive Link Text:Use meaningful text for links (e.g., “View Plant Details” instead of “Click Here”) so users understand where the link will take them.

Going Forward

Take away

Throughout this project, I deepened my understanding of the UI/UX design process—from initial user research to wireframing, prototyping, and testing. I learned how to align design decisions with real user needs by conducting usability research, such as user interviews, task-based testing, and observational studies. This helped me see how even small changes in layout or labeling can significantly improve the user experience.

Next Step

Moving forward, I would conduct additional usability tests with a broader and more diverse group of users to uncover further pain points and edge cases. Based on feedback, I would iterate on the interface design—especially enhancing the filtering system, improving mobile responsiveness, and adding tooltips or micro-interactions for better guidance.
